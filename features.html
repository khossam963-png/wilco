Features

What the crew actually does.

No mystery AI claims. Every line below is shipped, in beta38, and most of it is covered by a test you can run.

The flagship

The missile countdown

The server hook streams every in-flight weapon. WILCO measures the closure on the one guiding on you and speaks in bands: warn, crank at twelve seconds, BREAK at six — then the call that saves your energy: “missile trashed, you're clean, come back left.”

12s“Defend — missile inbound, right three o'clock, twelve seconds.”
08s“Crank left. Eight seconds.”
04s“BREAK RIGHT — FLARES — NOW.”
—“You're clean. Resume tasking.”

The brain

Deterministic. Offline. 44 milliseconds.

Exact math, not vibes

BRAA, pigeons, vectors, nine-lines — computed from the live feed, never invented. If the feed doesn't know, WILCO says so. Every number you hear can be checked against the mission.

OVERLORD> BRAA 042 / 18 / 22,000 / HOT

bearing · range · altitude · aspect — measured, not guessed

No API keys, ever

The default brain runs entirely on your PC. Optional cloud AI exists for richer improvisation — the offline brain feeds it the facts so it can't hallucinate.

The prediction engine

It flies the world forward: SAM ring entry warnings before you cross, merge countdowns, glideslope deviations, fuel playtime.

Readback verification

Runway, heading, altitude, squawk — checked in code. “Negative, runway three five right — read back.”

Mission memory

Kill tally across the shift, groups that faded and came back — “that group we lost is back.” Joker fuel remembered and nudged.

Wrong-freq redirects

Call Tower for taxi and you'll get sent to Ground — with the actual frequency spoken.

The ears

It understands you, not your spelling.

75 intents, 37,000+ phrasings

Say it formally, casually, or half-swallowed at 2 AM — no phrases to memorize. Certified on exams it had never seen, and the suites ship in the box.

Garble repair

Damerau–Levenshtein healing over an aviation lexicon: “pichure” → picture, “bogey dop” → bogey dope. 98.8% on real radio mangles.

Safety gate

Clearances are anchor-gated and confirm-tiered. Chatter can never trigger a takeoff clearance — hard-asserted in the test suite.

It learns YOUR phrases

Miss → rephrase → learned forever, per pilot. Your personal wording becomes part of its vocabulary.

Sub-second endpointing

It knows when you're done talking — and answers standard calls in about 0.9 seconds, mic to voice.

Compound calls

“Picture and pigeons” gets both answers in one transmission.

The voice

Sounds like a radio, not a robot.

A real comm-radio chain

Band-pass, presence peak, AGC, envelope-riding hiss, squelch tail — rebuilt from how radios actually distort, not a robot voice with static pasted on.

Different person per position

Ground doesn't sound like Tower. Overlord doesn't sound like Nail. Offline neural voices, no cloud.

Voice mood

Urgent calls speed up. Quiet nights relax. The pacing follows the situation.

Voice Studio

Paste an audio clip, train, assign — put any voice you own on any position, all locally.

The net

Disciplined with twelve pilots. Alive with one.

Mic triage

Every pending call is ranked — the pilot closest to dying gets the frequency first. The crew never steps on a human: the freq is re-checked the instant before it keys up.

Handoff memory
