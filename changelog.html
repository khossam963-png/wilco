Changelog

The sortie log.

Every beta, in order. Most of these came out of real flights — a pilot heard something wrong, we went and found out why.

Beta 40 — the Nail release

July 17, 2026CURRENT
  • Say "walk me in" and Nail runs your whole attack. He tests twelve run-in directions against the real battlefield — every other SAM and AAA ring on the way in and out, and the friendly positions, so your long rounds never point at friendlies — then flies you through it: steer to the roll-in, "turn in NOW", cleared hot the moment your nose is actually in the window, egress heading the second your weapon comes off.
  • Come off dry and he brings you back around for the re-attack. Target dies mid-run and he calls you off and re-tasks you. "Knock it off" cancels the run with a safe egress. One shooter at a time and the readback rules still apply.
  • MANPADS can't ambush you any more. Hard deck in the brief ("hard deck 12,000 — MANPADS in the box"), "get above 12000 NOW" if you sink below it — and a new ambush guard: orbit low over an undetected MANPAD with no attack running and no AWACS up, and Nail still calls you inside the ring and tells you to climb. That exact case used to be silent.
  • "How many left?" — the battle tracker: what's standing, your confirmed kills, what dies next. "What should I use?" — weapon advice read off your actual rails when the feed can see them ("you're carrying two by Maverick — launch from outside 3 miles").
  • All of it is proven by a new 43-check exam that flies the whole guided attack against live geometry. The radio card grew to 101 calls.

Beta 39 — the latency release

July 17, 2026
  • Five crew seats hear the same radio call — it now costs ONE transcription instead of five. On the bench the worst seat went from 2.9s to 0.6s; on real sorties, two out of three calls used to wait over two seconds during a busy net. Measured, fixed, and there's a stress test that proves it stays fixed.
  • A live LATENCY readout in the Control Station: hear (your voice to text) and think (brain to first word), measured from your own calls — not a marketing number.
  • No more double talk. The live log wrote some calls twice — yours and the crew's. Every field call now belongs to exactly one controller (Ground no longer parrots Tower's clearances), and only one seat logs an overheard transmission.
  • ATC that works the field: separation and sequencing between traffic, line-up-and-wait memory, traffic advisories, and phrase rotation so the crew stops repeating itself word for word.
  • New setup wizard — a guided first flight: checks, hearing, mic, the crew roster, and what to say first. Plus a Radio card button and a "didn't catch" card that shows what the crew missed so you can rephrase.
  • The radio-filter voice effect is back on for everyone (a config migration re-enables it), the crew keeps your callsign in every reply, and Stop all now also stops the SRS server it started.

Betas 34–38 — the radio week

July 15, 2026
  • The crew connects itself. WILCO joins your SRS server through External AWACS Mode — and if the server had it off, or the password didn't match, every controller was silently rejected and fell back to your PC speakers. Now WILCO configures the SRS server for you when it launches it: EAM on, password matched. Start the crew and the radio just connects.
  • Diagnose SRS. One button in the Control Station runs a full check against your SRS server and answers in plain English: is it reachable, does its version match, is External AWACS Mode on, is the password set — and what to do about each. There's a double-click SRS_DOCTOR.bat too.
  • It catches the split install. SRS auto-updates the client but can leave the server behind — the classic "worked yesterday, silent today" after an SRS update. WILCO now flags a server/client version split loudly, and automatically launches the NEWEST SRS server on your box so the split fixes itself.
  • No more claiming "connected" when the server said no. A controller used to print connected the moment the socket opened, even when SRS then rejected it. It now reads the server's real reply — and a mere version warning never drops the crew to speakers.
  • The support bundle finds your real Desktop (OneDrive redirects included) and opens Explorer with the file selected.

Beta 33 — the solo-sortie debrief

July 15, 2026
  • Every fix here came from one real sortie, written down in the cockpit. Nail now ranks the SAM's actual shooter first — the search radar is eyes, not the thing that shoots back — and "switch to the tank" re-tasks you instead of being heard as a frequency change.
  • A named spike correlates to the SAM you named: "Spike, SA-2" gets the SA-2 — notch and drag out of the ring — not the nearest MiG. Checking in inside a hostile SAM ring gets called out up front.
  • Reading back a clearance gets "readback correct" — Tower no longer re-issues the whole clearance at you.
  • Pin your callsign. DCS names every slot "New callsign" — set yours once in config and the crew always calls you by it (solo convenience: it steps aside the moment a second human is on the net).
  • The controllers vary their phrasing on the calls you hear most — taxi, takeoff, landing, inbound — instead of one canned line every time. The runway and hold-short words stay standard, so readbacks are unaffected.
  • Hook v10: the data feed survives a mission change without a DCS restart, and a dying unit can no longer flood the net with a hundred kill reports.

Beta 32 — the FPS release

July 14, 2026
  • WILCO now always loses the fight for CPU against DCS — as it should. Every WILCO process runs at below-normal Windows priority: a controller speaking or listening borrows cores DCS isn't using, instead of stealing frame time.
  • The voice engine is capped to 2 CPU threads (it grabbed every core per transmission before). Speech recognition capped to half your cores. Same hearing, same speed, half the fight.
  • The hearing stack no longer drags in gigabytes of unused ML libraries — lighter start, far lower memory use.
  • If the radio can't connect at startup (busy PC, SRS still loading), the crew retries quietly for up to 5 minutes instead of instantly switching to your PC speakers and open microphone.

Beta 31

July 14, 2026
  • The debrief release: every fix below came from one real sortie, analyzed call by call afterward.
  • Clean voices. WILCO's own radio effect is now off by default - SRS already applies its radio sound, so every voice was being processed twice into mud. (And MiG reads “mig” now, not “em eye gee”.)
  • One voice per warning. A missile launch is Overlord's call - not five controllers shouting the same thing. If the senior position is down, the next in line inherits it automatically.
  • The crew never parrots a garbled callsign: all positions share one callsign memory, and a mangled hearing folds into the name you actually use. “Copy Not, your fight” can never happen again.
  • Approach works arrivals like a real arrival controller - “request landing” gets vectors, never a ten-minute hold or a bounce to Tower mid-approach; vector altitudes raised; steering stops the moment Tower clears you to land.
  • “I'm NOT engaging” is a negative, never a confirmation. The living net stops repeating itself. Identical warnings never air twice back-to-back.

Beta 30

July 14, 2026
  • Talk to it like a person. A natural-speech certification pass: 99.4% understood on 100,000 plain-English asks (0.16% confidently wrong), 97.4% on a 1,000,000-utterance exam it never stored. Around 120 new everyday phrasings - “what does it look like up there”, “gear is down and locked”, “can we depart”, “steer me back to the field”.
  • Five deep engine fixes the new exams exposed - the word “like” could silently kill whole phrase families, “here fuel” could read as “refuel”, and a self-cancelling veto had muted every “say my heading” since it shipped. All caught, all covered by tests now.
  • License care. Invite codes now revalidate quietly in the background; a disabled code gets a polite radio notice and the Control Station says why - never dead air.
  • Radio card regenerated: 93 calls across 6 positions, matching everything above.

Beta 29

July 13, 2026
  • The Instructor. WILCO watches your real gauges now and coaches the two moments data can honestly judge: dragging it in low-and-slow (“you're behind the power curve — power up now, then set your pitch”) and the touchdown itself, graded to the foot-per-minute — from “greased it” to “have maintenance look at the gear.”
  • The Logbook. The crew remembers you across sorties — kills, landings and their trend, fuel habits. Check in and you might hear: “watch your gas tonight — that's three sorties running you've come home on fumes.”
  • Debrief with zero credits. The after-action report is now counted from the real tape — understood-first-time rate, a timeline with quotes, work-ons, crew reply speed — and needs no AI at all. The AI debrief still exists and falls back automatically.
  • Visual SAM reports. “Overlord, I can see an SA-2” is intel now: correlated with the scope (“good eyes — that's the SA-2 Guideline, bullseye…”) or plotted at your position and shared with every pilot who asks — flagged unconfirmed.
  • MAYDAY on every frequency. A real emergency once went unanswered on the AWACS freq — the mayday intent only lived on Tower and Approach. Every position catches it now, and answers like itself.
  • The small real-flight fixes: a bare hail gets “go ahead” instead of “say again”; “taxiing to the runway” gets “report holding short”; “I am in” works on Overlord and Nail; emergency phrasings can never be mis-learned; “laser OFF” can never fire the laser-ON intent.

Beta 28

July 13, 2026
  • The missile. Everyone says "missile in the air, defend" — DCS says it, the competition says it. It's the least useful true statement on the net, because it tells you nothing about whether you're about to die. WILCO tracks the missile, every second: "Missile off your six o'clock — twelve seconds. Notch and start defending." … "Still tracking, eight seconds. Get your nose off." … "BREAK RIGHT! FLARES! NOW!" … "Missile's off — you're clean. Nicely flown." A live countdown on the real weapon, the break called at the instant it works, and — just as important — being told you're clean, so you can stop bleeding energy and turn back. No AI can do this. It would have to invent the twelve.
  • The net is alive. Fly solo and you used to hear nothing unless you keyed the mic. Now the field works other traffic around you — and every call is a real aircraft in your mission, at its real position. It switches itself off the moment a second human joins: a squadron's net is already alive.
  • It sounds like a radio. The band-pass was a brick-wall filter, which rings — that hollow, synthetic quality. Rebuilt with a proper rolloff, a presence peak at 2 kHz (what your ear recognises as "a radio" before it understands a word), real AGC, and hiss that rides under the voice instead of sitting on top of it.
  • It answers in under a second. ~1.6s → ~0.9s. The brain was never the problem (44 ms). Hearing was. It now runs a model 2.8× faster that, measured on real calls, is understood identically — because the fuzzy engine was built to heal exactly that garble.
  • It doesn't step on you. It re-checks the frequency immediately before keying up, and waits for the net to settle — so a breath mid-sentence is never mistaken for "he's finished". With a squadron the mic is triaged: the man closest to dying gets it.
  • Nail runs a flight. He'd have cleared two strikers hot onto the same target at the same time. Now: one shooter, the rest hold high in cover, and the wingman is called in when the lead comes off.
  • And the things that were silently broken: the hook had never once reported a kill (the handler was a dead stub — so "splash one" could never have fired); an Su-27 flying with a MiG-29 was called a MiG-29, the Flanker vanishing from the picture; the ATIS letter was a hash that jumped from Kilo to Delta and back; and the runway rule was corrupting MGRS grids in a 9-line.
  • Restart DCS once — the hook is new.

Beta 27

July 13, 2026
  • Every controller had holes — they're filled. We audited the calls a pilot makes on a normal sortie and found 7 of 13 weren't understood at all. Some that did "work" were worse than a miss: "request line up and wait" was being answered with CLEARED FOR TAKEOFF. Line-up-and-wait is the opposite of a takeoff clearance — you hold on the runway. That's a runway incursion waiting to happen. It's now its own call, and it's safety-gated.
  • Tower — line up and wait, the option / touch-and-go / closed traffic, and the military overhead break ("initial", "break at midfield").
  • Ground — pushback, and a real IFR departure clearance in CRAFT order: cleared as fragged, climb and maintain, departure frequency, squawk.
  • Approach — holding, with a fix, a turn direction and an expect-further-clearance time.
  • Overlord — the ROE ("are we weapons free?"), and commit ("committing north group" used to be read as a question about groups).
  • Nail verifies your 9-line readback. Real JTAC doctrine: the striker reads back lines 4, 6 and 8 before anyone says "cleared hot". Nail never checked — he'd brief you and just clear you. Now he won't clear you hot until the elevation is right. A wrong one puts the bomb short, or long into the friendlies.
  • The exam doubled to 100,000 sentences — and got harder. It now speaks non-native English ("can you give me the picture", "i need to know the weather") — the way half the playerbase actually talks — plus terse radio habits and speech-to-text garble. Two exemptions were also removed that had been quietly hiding failures. Result: 95.9% understood, of 100,000 sentences stored nowhere. The exam paid for itself, catching "touch and go" filed under the wrong intent, and "no more missiles" being answered with a defensive notch instead of being sent home.

Beta 26

July 12, 2026
  • Real instrument approaches — at every airfield, on every map. WILCO now reads the actual navaids out of DCS's own map files (38 ILS localizers on Sinai alone). Approach clears you for the real ILS and reads you the real localizer frequency. If the active runway hasn't got one, it says so and vectors you to a visual — it will never invent a needle that isn't there.
  • Nail is a JTAC you can talk to. He used to just read a 9-line at you. Ask him "what's down there?" and he answers off the live battlefield: "An SA-8 Gecko, two T-72s and a Ural in the box. The SA-8 is your problem — your 12 o'clock, 9 miles." Ask what to hit first and he tells you why. Ask if it's defended, how far it reaches, whether you're already inside it, and what run-in keeps the frag off the friendlies. And "say BDA" now reads the sim's real kill feed — "good hits, one T-72 destroyed" — instead of "stand by for BDA", forever.
  • "Spiked" is a defensive call again. Being locked used to get you a bogey dope to read while you died. Now: "Spike correlates MiG-29, BRAA zero two seven, 16, hot. Notch right, heading one one seven. Call defending."
  • It answers your readback. "Copy, taxiing to the runway" used to get silence — so you'd say the whole thing over again. On Ground, Tower and Approach the call right after a clearance is now taken as the readback it obviously is, even with no numbers in it.
  • Splash. Kill something and the crew calls it: "Warhawk, splash one. MiG-29 destroyed — good kill."
  • It understands more of what you say. Straight from real flight logs: the word "repeat" was in no intent at all, and "holding short runway 35R" — a completely standard call — Ground could not even hear. It also stopped teaching itself wrong answers: the phrase-learner had quietly bound "repeat" to bogey dope.

Beta 25

July 12, 2026
  • The two brains became one crew. WILCO has always had two: an offline engine that computes the fight exactly, and an AI that talks like a person. They used to be either/or. Now the engine reads your call first — through the static — works out the answer from the live mission, and hands it to the AI, whose only remaining job is to say it. It stops guessing what you said, stops doing arithmetic, and cannot invent a number. Less to work out means it's on the air faster.
  • That includes the missile call. When a SAM fires, the AI now transmits the engine's exact line. It can no longer make up the bearing on a missile that is already in the air.
  • The 9-line stopped lying about the friendlies. Line 8 was being measured to your own aircraft — so the brief put the friendlies wherever you happened to be flying. It is now the real friendly ground unit, and it calls DANGER CLOSE when they are inside a mile of the target.
  • The grid is readable. "Line six: three six Romeo, Uniform Uniform, three six eight five, seven five four two." It used to say "three thousand six hundred eighty-five" — which is how you type the wrong square into the jet.
  • "Runway three five right" — not "runway three five arr." Every clearance, every ATIS.
  • You can tell the groups apart. Two contacts on the same bearing were both called "the northeast group". Now they're lead group and trail group — and a hot group inside twenty miles gets the word it has earned: "Threat."
  • The SAM warning says "SA-2 Guideline" instead of spelling out the Russian designator, letter by letter, in the middle of a launch call.

Beta 24

July 11, 2026
  • The crew can see YOU. DCS gives most pilots a default pilot name, so the callsign you said matched nothing on the scope — and everything that needs your position ("bogey dope", "declare", "BRAA") answered "I don't hold you on the link". WILCO now binds the callsign you speak to your jet. And since speech-to-text mangles a callsign differently every time, the controllers vote — the name you say most becomes your name.
  • It calls the missile. The DCS hook was seeing incoming SAMs and discarding them: launches were filtered by a guessed list that had never been tested against a real shot. A SAM fired at a pilot and the radio stayed silent. Missing a launch is the worst thing this program can do — the filter is gone. (Restart DCS once so the new hook loads.)
  • It answers your readback. "Taxiing runway three five, copy" was being swallowed as a plain "roger", so readbacks got silence — and a wrong readback was never caught. Now: "readback correct. Contact Tower two five two decimal zero when ready."
  • "Ground picture" answered with bandits in the air; it now gives surface threats. "I can see an SA-2" is understood. And a station or airfield name can never be mistaken for your callsign again.

Beta 23

July 11, 2026
  • Offline brain, guaranteed everywhere. A full "stranger test" — fresh download, fresh PC, no coaching — caught the text-mode crew and the controller-initiated calls skipping the offline brain. Both now answer instantly with no internet and no account, same as the voice crew.
  • Launch and SAM warnings can never silently skip again — the engine now shouts in the log if any part of the crew runs unwired.
  • Practice mode fixed: the fake-traffic sim now reaches every controller, so "no DCS" test flights get a live scope, not a dark one.
  • No more window flash on Start all / Restart / Stop — everything launches fully hidden.

Beta 22

July 11, 2026
  • Seat-aware plans. Paid plans serve the licensed number of pilots per session; an extra pilot gets one polite notice — and safety calls are never gated: launch warnings, go-arounds and traffic reach every pilot, seat or no seat.
  • Voice Studio fix: the music remover now works on machines without FFmpeg (it silently skipped separation before).
  • The panel shows your plan and seats; the self-learning loop now mines only real flights (test noise excluded).

Beta 21

July 11, 2026
  • The brain doubled its vocabulary — 11,468 radio phrasings across 72 kinds of call (was 5,684): more natural registers, more plain-English shapes, more milsim brevity.
  • Re-proven against 50,000 calls it had never seen: 97.6% understood. All 8 test suites green; understanding still takes under 100 ms.

Beta 20

July 11, 2026
  • Play-tested by flying a full sortie against it — and fixing what only a real flight finds: multiplayer callsigns without digits ("Warhawk") now resolve with several pilots on frequency, fighters are never sent to the JTAC for tasking, runway corrections say the full runway ("three five right").
  • Stress-hardened. A new adversarial suite proves it: prompt-injection, corrupted mission data, flooding, stale confirms — no crashes, and no trick can ever produce a clearance.

Betas 15–19 — the ELITE run

July 11, 2026
  • Continuous approach control. "Request vectors" now means being worked: corrections as you drift, the intercept turn, the Tower handoff — "I'll call your turns."
  • Tactical judgment. Ask "should I take this fight?" and Overlord counts the bandits, weighs your altitude, aspect and fuel, and answers with the reasons out loud.
  • Smart clarification. A genuinely ambiguous call gets "picture, or bogey dope?" instead of "say again".
  • It learns you. Miss once, rephrase once — your personal phrasing works forever after, per pilot, across sessions.
  • Intercept geometry. Cutoff vectors that lead the bandit, closure rate and a countdown to the merge in every dope call.
  • Real brevity doctrine. Groups inside 25 miles come as BRAA off your nose; bullseye stays for the far picture. Multi-ships are addressed as a flight.
  • Shift memory. Joker fuel tracked with a nudge ten minutes later, kill tallies in the end-of-shift call, returning groups called as "the same customers".
  • Sub-second replies — it answers the moment your call is complete, plus crosswind warnings, compound calls ("picture and pigeons"), and a red status banner in the Control Station that names silent failures.

Betas 9–14 — the local brain

July 9–11, 2026
  • The brain moved onto your PC. Understanding, tactics and voices now run 100% locally — no cloud, no API keys, no internet needed. Cloud AI became an optional switch.
  • 5,684 phrasings, 69 kinds of call — grown from real flight transcripts and proven against 50,000 calls it had never seen (97.5% understood).
  • Garble repair. "Bogey dop", "taxy", "earth formation alpha" — healed before matching. Hesitations ("uh", "you know") ignored like a human ear does.
  • Readbacks verified in code — get a number wrong and exactly that number is corrected. Safety clearances confirm first when your radio was shaky.
  • Missile launch warnings that actually work — a new weapon watcher sees every launch the moment it exists (the old DCS event bridge never fired).
  • Voice Studio. Clone any voice from a sample recording, train it on your own GPU with a live step counter, assign it to any controller. Nothing uploads.
  • Tacview telemetry feed for multiplayer servers where you can't install the hook, and a printable kneeboard radio card generated from the brain itself.

Beta 4

  • One law for all of them: cool = fewer words. Personality lives in how they say the call, never in extra sentences.
