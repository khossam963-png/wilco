Pricing questions
How do "seats" work exactly?
One person — the host — runs WILCO. A seat is a pilot the crew talks to during a session: everyone on your SRS frequencies who checks in. The first pilots to call take the seats; if an extra pilot calls in, the crew tells them politely — once — that the net is full, and that's it. Safety is never gated: missile launch warnings, go-arounds and traffic calls go to every pilot on the net, seat or no seat. Seats free up every session.
What happens to my beta code when plans launch?
It keeps working — it just gets a plan attached. Subscribe with the same email you signed up with and your code, your settings and your learned phrasings all carry over. Beta pilots keep the founding price for as long as they stay subscribed.
Do the bigger plans get smarter AI?
No — and that's a promise. Every plan runs the identical brain, voices and features. Seats are the only difference. We think paywalling intelligence is how you get a crew nobody trusts.
How do payments work?
Checkout runs through our payment partner — international cards and popular local payment methods are accepted, and the receipt lands in your email. Your card details never touch WILCO's servers. Cancel anytime from the same checkout portal; you keep access until the end of the period you paid for.
Do I need to be online to fly?
Flying is fully offline — the brain and voices live on your PC. The crew does a quick license check when it starts and there's a grace window, so a flaky connection never grounds you mid-campaign.
Can I upgrade mid-month?
Yes — upgrades take effect immediately and your remaining days are credited toward the bigger plan. Downgrades apply at your next renewal.
Is there a refund policy?
If WILCO doesn't work for your setup in the first 14 days, write to support and you get your money back. No forms, no fight — we'd rather have the crash report than the fifteen dollars.
