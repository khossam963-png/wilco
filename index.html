Understanding · no magic words

Say it your way.
Even badly.

Textbook brevity, plain English, or a mangled transmission through a hot mic — the brain hears through it. Proven against 50,000 calls it had never seen, hesitations and static included.

GARBLE "earth formation alpha"

Repaired to information alpha, understood, taxi clearance issued. Transpositions, dropped letters, homophones — all healed before matching.

PLAIN ENGLISH "where's the closest enemy?"

Works exactly like the brevity call — BRAA to the nearest hostile, with closure rate and time to the merge when it's a real intercept.

AMBIGUOUS a half-readable call

It asks — "picture, or bogey dope?" — instead of guessing or going silent. One answer later, it acts.

COMPOUND "picture and pigeons"

Both answered in one transmission, like a controller who actually listened to the whole sentence.

YOURS your own weird phrasing

Miss once, rephrase once — it maps your version to the right call and remembers it forever, per pilot, across sessions.

Fair questions
Does it really run without internet?
Yes. The brain is fully local — understanding, tactics and voices all run on your PC, no API keys, no cloud calls mid-flight. There's an optional cloud-AI mode you can switch on in the Control Station for extra improvisation, but the default needs nothing.
Will it fight my GPU while DCS is running?
No. Hearing defaults to a CPU model precisely so DCS keeps the whole graphics card. On a strong GPU you can switch to the bigger ears in one click.
What happens when it mishears me?
It repairs garble ("bogey dop" works), asks a clarifying question when a call is genuinely ambiguous, and demands a confirm before anything dangerous — a noisy mic can never clear you onto a runway. Every miss is logged in the panel and learned from.
Does it work in multiplayer?
Yes — only the host runs WILCO; your flight just tunes the same SRS frequencies and talks. No hook access on the server? The Tacview telemetry feed gives the crew eyes without installing anything server-side.
What do I need?
Windows 10/11, DCS World, and SRS (which you already use). Any mic. No accounts, no API keys, no third-party subscriptions.
Is my voice or audio uploaded anywhere?
No. Speech recognition, the brain and voice training all run locally. Voice Studio recordings stay on your disk, full stop.
What will it cost after the beta?
From $15/month for a two-ship, scaling by how many pilots fly on your net — never by features. Every plan gets the identical brain, voices and updates. Beta pilots keep the founding price. The full breakdown is on the pricing page.
Which aircraft does it work with?
All of them — the crew reads the live mission, not your cockpit. It even tailors tasking to your airframe: strikers get pointed at the JTAC, fighters get the picture and commits.
